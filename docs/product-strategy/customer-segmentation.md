# Customer Segmentation

## Primary Persona: Solo Entrepreneur / One-Person Company

Runs an entire business alone — freelance consultant, agency-of-one, micro-SaaS founder, indie creator. Wears every hat: product, sales, delivery, and marketing. May or may not be technical, but is resourceful and willing to try AI tools. Has no content marketing infrastructure and no budget for agencies. Measured on revenue, inbound leads, and staying visible in their niche.

**Pain points:**
- **Invisible to AI agents** — When prospects ask ChatGPT "who's the best [their niche] consultant/tool," competitors with better-structured content get cited. They don't know why or how to fix it.
- **Time poverty** — Every hour on content is an hour not billing clients or building product. Content always loses to revenue-generating work.
- **No content system** — Publishes in bursts (launch week, a random LinkedIn post) then goes silent for months. No pipeline, no cadence, no compounding.
- **Can't afford agencies** — $2K/post agencies are out of reach. Freelancers ($500/post) still require briefing and editing that eats the day.
- **Curious about AI but lost on AEO** — Has heard of AI-generated answers citing brands but hasn't connected the dots that content structure determines whether AI surfaces their business.

**What they'd buy:**
- A content system that makes their business show up when AI agents answer questions in their niche
- Something they can set up once and monitor weekly, not a service they have to manage
- Clear ROI framing: cost per post, hours saved, citation visibility over time
- Feels like a tool, not a retainer — they're doers, not delegators

**Trigger event:** They ask ChatGPT about their own niche and a competitor shows up instead. Or they see a competitor's content cited in a Perplexity answer and realize it's a structural advantage, not a writing quality advantage.

## Secondary Persona: Small Marketing Team (2-5 people)

A lean content or marketing team inside a growing company — like Jason's team at V2Cloud. Has a content marketing manager, maybe a writer or SEO person, but not enough bandwidth to cover every channel. They publish regularly but struggle with consistency, AEO, and scaling output without hiring more.

- **Industries**: B2B SaaS, cloud/IT services, dev tools, professional services
- **Budget**: $1K-$5K/month on content (mix of in-house writing and occasional freelancers)
- **Trigger**: Realizing AI-generated answers cite competitors who publish more structured, AEO-optimized content — and their team doesn't have the bandwidth or knowledge to close the gap
- **Key difference from primary**: They have people and some budget, but need a system to multiply their output and get into AI citations without hiring another full-time writer. They want leverage, not headcount.

## Out of Scope

- Non-business solo creators (hobbyist bloggers, personal journalers) — no buying intent
- Enterprise content teams (10+ people) — they have agencies and in-house writers, they need AEO consulting not a pipeline
- E-commerce / DTC brands — product content is fundamentally different from thought leadership content
