# Content Topic Pillars

Four pillars for openclaws.blog. Every post maps to at least one. All topics must be framed as problems our personas — solo entrepreneurs and small marketing teams — would actually search for.

## 1. Hiring an AI Content Agent, Cost and Quality

Comparing DIY vs freelancer vs agency vs AI content pipeline. Real pricing at every stage: what a solo entrepreneur spending $0-$2K/month should expect, what a small marketing team budgeting $1K-$5K/month gets. Cost-per-post math, quality trade-offs, when automation pays for itself vs when you still need a human editor.

## 2. Growing Organic Visibility Without a Marketing Hire

How to build inbound traffic and leads when you're the only marketer (or your team is stretched thin). Publishing cadence on autopilot, content repurposing (blog → social → newsletter), SEO basics that move the needle without a dedicated SEO person. Practical tactics for founders and lean teams, not agency playbooks.

## 3. Getting Cited by AI

How ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews choose who to cite. Structured content, earned media distribution, citation monitoring, Answer Engine Optimization (AEO). The goal: when someone asks an AI about your niche, your business comes up — not your competitor's.

## 4. New Tools and Advances in AI Content Pipelines

What's changing in the tools and workflows that power AI-assisted content — and what it means for your publishing pipeline and AI citation chances. New models, agent frameworks, publishing integrations, and workflow patterns. Filtered through the lens of: does this help a solo entrepreneur or small team publish better content and get cited more?
