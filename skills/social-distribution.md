# Social Distribution

Amplify blog content by sharing to social platforms. All posts require user approval before publishing.

## Approval Workflow

**Never post without confirmation.** Always:
1. Draft the social post
2. Send the draft to the user via Telegram (or show in REPL)
3. Wait for explicit approval before posting
4. Post only after receiving confirmation

## Platform Playbooks

### Reddit

Best subreddits for our content:
- **r/LocalLLaMA** -- Technical posts about local AI, model comparisons
- **r/selfhosted** -- Self-hosting guides, infrastructure posts
- **r/artificial** -- Broader AI industry pieces
- **r/commandline** -- CLI tool posts, developer workflow content
- **r/opensource** -- Open-source project announcements, community posts

**Posting guidelines:**
- Read subreddit rules before posting (check sidebar/wiki)
- Use descriptive titles, not clickbait
- Self-promotion rules: most subreddits limit self-promotion to ~10% of your posts
- Prefer text posts with value upfront, link to blog at the end
- Best times: weekday mornings US time (9-11am ET)
- Engage with comments after posting

**Title formats that work:**
- "I built X to solve Y" (Show & Tell)
- "Guide: How to do X with Y" (Tutorial)
- "X vs Y vs Z: My experience after N months" (Comparison)

### X / Twitter

**Voice & tone:**
- Write like a business owner talking to another business owner over coffee
- Simple English — no jargon, no buzzwords, no "leverage" or "synergize"
- Short sentences. Punchy. Conversational.
- Use concrete numbers and comparisons people can feel ("costs less per month than hiring a contractor for one day")
- End with a take or opinion, not a generic CTA

**Single tweet format (preferred):**
- Lead with the insight or a surprising fact — never lead with the link
- Use everyday analogies ("That's like running your accounting software on a personal phone")
- One idea per tweet — don't cram multiple points
- Skip hashtags unless highly relevant (1-2 max)
- No emojis unless they add clarity
- Under 280 chars, use line breaks for readability

**Thread format for blog posts:**
1. Hook tweet: One surprising stat or contrarian take from the post
2. 3-5 tweets, each making ONE point in plain language
3. Each tweet should stand alone — someone skimming should get value from any single tweet
4. Final tweet: Link to full post, no "like and retweet" asks
5. Keep each tweet under 280 chars

**What works:**
- "X costs less than Y" (concrete cost comparisons)
- "Most businesses do X. Here's why that breaks." (challenge assumptions)
- "The difference between X and Y? [one-line answer]" (simple contrasts)
- Relatable problems → simple solutions

**What doesn't work:**
- Marketing speak ("unlock the power of", "game-changing", "revolutionary")
- Vague claims without numbers
- Threads that are just a blog post copy-pasted into tweets
- Leading with "I'm excited to announce"

### Hacker News

**Submission tips:**
- Title: Use the exact blog post title (HN penalizes editorialized titles)
- Best times: 6-9am ET weekdays
- "Show HN:" prefix for product launches or demos
- Don't ask for upvotes -- it's against HN rules
- Be ready to engage in comments (HN rewards participation)

## Cross-Linking

After publishing social posts:
- Add social links back to the blog post as a comment or update
- Link between platforms ("Discussion on HN: [link]", "Reddit thread: [link]")

## Draft Templates

### Reddit Post Draft
```
Title: [descriptive, non-clickbait title]

[2-3 sentence summary of the key insight]

[Main value from the blog post -- don't just tease, provide substance]

Full post: [blog URL]

---
Happy to answer any questions!
```

### Twitter Thread Draft
```
1/ [Surprising stat or contrarian take. Make them stop scrolling.]

2/ [The problem in plain English. Use an analogy if it helps.]

3/ [What most people get wrong about this.]

4/ [The simple fix or shift in thinking. Use a concrete number.]

5/ Full breakdown here: [URL]
```

### Twitter Single Tweet Draft
```
[One surprising fact or simple comparison.]

[One sentence explaining why it matters to a business owner.]
```

### HN Submission Draft
```
Title: [exact blog post title]
URL: [blog post URL]
```
